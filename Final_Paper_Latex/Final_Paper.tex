\section{Introduction}
This document provides a rigorous evaluation of the \textbf{Fama-French 5-Factor Model} using R. The model extends the Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM) by incorporating Size, Value, Profitability, and Investment factors alongside standard market risk \parencite{fama1993common, fama2015five}. The core objective is to determine the efficacy of these five factors in explaining the excess returns of a portfolio comprising eight selected assets.

\subsection{Objectives}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Empirical Analysis:} Assess how well the five factors (Market, SMB, HML, RMW, CMA) explain asset returns.
    \item \textbf{Statistical Interpretation:} Analyze alpha and beta coefficients for statistical significance and economic meaning.
\end{itemize}

\section{Methodology}
The methodology involves a systematic technical workflow utilizing the Kenneth French data library \parencite{french2024data} and R-based statistical modeling.

\subsection{Asset Selection}
The following 8 assets were selected representing tilts toward Market, SMB, HML, RMW, and CMA. Each selection is justified based on historical factor exposure.

\subsection{Descriptive Statistics}
Historical return data for the selected assets is analyzed using mean, median, standard deviation, skewness, and kurtosis to understand the distribution and risk profile of the portfolio.
